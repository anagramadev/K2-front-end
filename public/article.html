Obstbauer im Südosten Englands produziert eigenen Strom mit 1,5 MWp Aufdachanlage

02 march 2022 Produkt

Share

Von der Auslegung in K2 Base bis hin zur Installation unserer Montagesysteme für alle Dachtypen von Ziegeldach, Trapezblech oder Flachdach kann unser K2 Team Ihnen Ihre Fragen beantworten.

‘Renewable energy is a crucial component in the UK’s plans to be a carbon-neutral economy by 2050, and farming and the topfruit industry have an important role to play in helping the government to achieve this,’ says Ross Goatham. AC Goatham & Son grows more than 350 million apples and about 55 million pears per year on its total of 25 farms.

Shaun Beattie, director at BeBa Energy added: ‘Working in partnership with the team at Goatham’s to develop this project was a pleasure. If the UK is to achieve its ambitions of net zero by 2050, then business must play their part. This investment from Goatham’s shows just how seriously they take their responsibilities!’

With the 541.28 kWp, the metal processing company can cover part of its electricity needs (source: Greenmile).

A total of 1,604 modules are mounted on the 5° pitched trapezoidal sheet metal roof using the MiniRail System.

Installation information

  • Location: County of Kent, United Kingdom
  • Building: Farm
  • Commissioning: January 2021
  • Nominal output: 1,510.62 kWp
  • Annual yield: 1.33 million kWh
  • CO2 savings: 293.121 tons (7,328 trees)
  • System: Solar Fastener with second rail layer of SolidRail Light + SingleRail
  • Special feature: steel purlins
  • Number of modules: 4,443
  • modulesUse of electricity: own consumption for packaging and cooling
  • Partner: BeBa Energy
